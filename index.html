How to Play YAHTZEE

On the outside, YAHTZEE may look like a simple dice game.

However, on the inside, it is pretty convoluted.

To play YAHTZEE in real life, you'll need five six-sided dice, a writing utensil, and a YAHTZEE scorecard.

On a normal YAHTZEE scorecard, you'll find thirteen unique scoring combos spanning two sections.

The Lower Section is much more convoluted than the Upper Section.

How to Score in the Upper Section

ACES - Score Aces only.

TWOS - Score Twos only.

THREES - Score Threes only.

FOURS - Score Fours only.

FIVES - Score Fives only.

SIXES - Score Sixes only.

BONUS - Score 35 here if you scored 63 or higher in your Upper Section.

How to Score in the Lower Section

TRIPLET - Score total of all dice IF you have three of one number (e.g., 2-2-2-3-6). Otherwise score zero.

QUADRUPLET - Score total of all dice IF you have four of one number (e.g., 6-6-6-6-3). Otherwise score zero.

FULL HOUSE - Score 25 IF you have three of one number and two of another (e.g., 4-4-5-5-5). Otherwise score zero.

S STRAIGHT - Score 30 IF you have a sequence of four numbers (e.g., 1-2-3-4). Otherwise score zero.

L STRAIGHT - Score 40 IF you have a sequence of five numbers (e.g., 2-3-4-5-6). Otherwise score zero.

CHANCE - Score total of all dice, no matter what. This is useful if you have a roll that doesn't score anything. However, you only get one - use it wisely!

YAHTZEE - Score 50 IF you have five of one number (e.g., 5-5-5-5-5). Otherwise score zero.

BONUS - Got another YAHTZEE? No problem! If the number you rolled a YAHTZEE with is open in the Upper Section, score there. (For example, if you rolled 4-4-4-4-4 and you have Fours open, score there.) If the number you rolled a YAHTZEE with has already been scored, you can score any Lower Section combo that hasn't been scored already.

Maximum Possible Score

The maximum possible score in one game of YAHTZEE is 1,575.

This is only achievable if you roll a YAHTZEE on every turn!

Click here to view the breakdown of the maximum possible score!

On Each Turn

FIRST ROLL: You must roll all five dice.

At this point, when you roll all five dice for the first time of a turn, you may choose to "hold" one or more dice, or you may choose to reroll all five dice for the second roll. Holding a die retains its value for the next roll.

All held dice are automatically unheld upon execution of the third and final roll of a given turn.

If you manage to roll a YAHTZEE on the first roll of a given turn (which the odds of that happening are 1 in 1,296), score it right away! YAHTZEEs are the best combo to get in terms of big-time scoring! You could score up to 1,250 points from YAHTZEEs alone in one game!

If you didn't roll a YAHTZEE on the first roll, that's OK! You have up to three rolls per turn.

SECOND ROLL: Didn't get the combo you wanted on the first roll? No problem! Roll everything again! Got a part of the combo you wanted? Hold a few dice and reroll the rest! You may choose to hold more dice on this roll, or you may also choose to unhold dice that were held in the first roll.

THIRD ROLL: Once you roll the dice for the third time of a turn, it automatically becomes time to score. Use the scoring rules above and place your scores in any combo that has not been scored already. All held dice are automatically unheld upon execution of this final roll.

Keyboard Shortcuts

Space - Roll all unheld dice.

T - Open the "How to Play" menu.

C - Open the Changelog.

/ - Open this menu.

1-5 - Hold dice.

N - Start a new game.

P - Place your score in the selected combination on the scorecard.

Q - Perform a hard reset and refresh the page. Beware: this will wipe your game history!

R - Refresh the page.

G - View previous games.

H - Hold all dice.

U - Unhold all dice.

I - Invert your selection of held dice.
